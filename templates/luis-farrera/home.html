What Makes The Work Different

Three non-negotiables.The Method

01 / Saturation

Engineered For Year Ten

Color choice starts with how a pigment ages — not how it looks at the consultation. Every palette is tested against your skin tone and placement so the piece still reads bold after healing, sun exposure, and time.

02 / Composition

Industrial Design Foundation

Honors-level industrial design training is the quiet edge. Balance, negative space, and flow come from architecture — not tattoo flash. Pieces feel anchored to the body, not stuck onto it.

03 / Process

One Client Per Day

Private Soho studio, appointment-only, single-client schedule. No rushed sessions, no chair-flipping, no ego. The day is yours — consultation, rest, and the work get the time they require.

The Artist

Caracas → Miami → NYCLuis Farrera

Caracas, 2005. Seventeen years old, winning graffiti competitions on the side of industrial design school. That street-to-studio pipeline is still the blueprint — color with intention, composition with structure.

A decade in Miami sharpened the palette. Then 2020 brought him to New York for good. Today he works out of a private Soho studio at 132 Crosby St — one client per day, one appointment at a time, for people who understand the difference between getting a tattoo and commissioning one.
