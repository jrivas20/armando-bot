Behind the Art
THE PIECE
THAT CHANGED
HOW I WORK
What I am actually trying to do with every client.
 
 
 
Hi {{contact.first_name}},
A client came to me with a photograph of a flower her grandmother used to grow in the garden. She did not want a copy of the photo. She wanted to feel something when she looked at it.
We spent two consultations getting the concept right. The composition changed three times. The color palette went through four versions. By the time we began, I knew that piece better than almost any other I had done.
What came out of that session was not just a tattoo. It was a translation of a memory into something permanent. That is a different kind of responsibility.
That session changed how I approach every brief. I stopped thinking about what looks good and started asking what it needs to feel like.
That is what I am trying to do with every client. Not reproduce something. Translate something. If you have a concept with a story behind it, that is exactly the kind of work I want to be doing.
