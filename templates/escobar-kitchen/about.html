The Escobar Kitchen Story

Where Two Worlds
Meet On A Plate

The Escobar Kitchen didn't start with a business plan. It started with a question: what happens when you stop choosing between Latin and Asian food and just cook both, together, without apology? The answer was bold. Unexpected. And Orlando couldn't get enough of it.

Founded in 2019, we set out to create a restaurant that felt like two cultures sitting down at one table. Latin warmth, big spices, and the comfort of street food you grew up with — fused with Japanese technique, clean precision, and the kind of flavor balance you usually only find in fine dining. That combination? That's The Escobar Kitchen.

We started with one location in Hunters Creek. The response was immediate. Word spread. Within a few years, we opened in Lake Nona and then Downtown Orlando. Today we serve thousands of regulars across Central Florida — people who come back not just for the food, but for what it represents: bold fusion done right, with no shortcuts and no compromise.

And we do it all while letting you skip the delivery app fees. Order direct, pickup in 15 minutes, taste the difference. That's the promise we've kept since day one.
