Implant-Grade Jewelry

ONLY THE
BEST IN.

Cheap jewelry is the #1 cause of piercing complications. We only use implant-grade materials — so your body can heal without interference.

Implant-Grade Titanium
ASTM F136 titanium — the gold standard for new piercings. Completely biocompatible, anodized to any color, lightweight, and the safest choice for fresh placements.
Solid Gold
14k and 18k solid gold — no plating, no fill. Safe for all skin types, hypoallergenic, and perfect for anyone who wants timeless luxury in their jewelry.
Surgical Steel
ASTM F138 implant-grade steel — nickel content meets ISO 10993 biocompatibility standards. A cost-effective, proven choice for healed piercings.
Niobium
Hypoallergenic and nickel-free — the go-to for clients with metal sensitivities. As safe as titanium with a softer, more matte aesthetic.

Before You Come In

PIERCING
FAQ.

Does it hurt? What should I expect?
Piercing pain varies by placement and individual pain tolerance. Most piercings are a brief sharp sensation — under 2 seconds. Lobes are the gentlest; cartilage and body piercings are slightly more intense. Our piercers talk you through every step so there are no surprises.
How long does a piercing take to heal?
Healing timelines vary by placement: lobes 6–8 weeks, cartilage 6–12 months, nostril 4–6 months, septum 6–8 weeks, navel 6–12 months, nipple 9–12 months. These are minimums — don't change jewelry early. We'll tell you exactly what to expect at your placement.
What jewelry do you use for a fresh piercing?
All initial piercings are done with implant-grade titanium (ASTM F136) or solid gold — never surgical steel for fresh piercings. We use longer jewelry initially to accommodate swelling, then downsize at your follow-up once healing is underway.
Can I bring my own jewelry?
We generally recommend against it. Jewelry purchased outside a professional piercing studio may not meet implant-grade standards, could be the wrong gauge or length, and typically can't be autoclaved properly. If you have a specific piece, bring it in and we'll assess it honestly.
How should I clean my piercing?
Twice daily with a sterile saline wound wash spray (0.9% sodium chloride, no additives). Let it air dry. Do not rotate the jewelry — this is outdated advice that causes irritation. Avoid alcohol, hydrogen peroxide, antibacterial soap, and Neosporin on the piercing.
Do you pierce infants and children?
Yes — we pierce infants (ear lobes only) and minors with proper parental consent. A legal guardian must be present with valid photo ID. If the guardian's last name differs from the child's, a notarized consent document is required. See our full minor policy above.
Do I need an appointment or can I walk in?
Walk-ins are welcome at both Kissimmee and Deltona locations during business hours. For complex placements or if you're bringing a group, feel free to call ahead — but it's not required. We'll get you in and out efficiently.
